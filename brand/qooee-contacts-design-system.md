# Qooee Contacts Design System

Status: corrected brand guideline for Qooee Contacts.

This document defines the Qooee master brand and its first product, Qooee Contacts. It uses the supplied logo assets and the supplied primary, secondary, and tertiary colour palettes as source material. Do not change the master brand system without a formal brand decision.

## 1. Brand Foundation

### Brand Role

Qooee makes sensible, enjoyable productivity tools for professionals.

The brand should feel useful, calm, well-made, and quietly distinctive. It should not feel like social media, a CRM, or another attention-seeking productivity app.

### First Product

Qooee Contacts is a networked address book for professionals.

It helps trusted contacts keep each other's details up to date automatically.

### Core Idea

Quiet professional infrastructure.

Qooee helps professional relationships work without making people perform their network.

### Value Proposition

Qooee keeps professional contact details current, without social noise.

### Positioning Statement

For professionals who need useful networks but dislike social media, Qooee Contacts is a networked address book that keeps trusted contact details up to date. Unlike LinkedIn, contact apps, or CRM tools, Qooee is built for mutual accuracy and quiet utility, not visibility, lead capture, or engagement.

### What Qooee Is Not

Qooee is not:

- a social media product
- a feed
- a personal branding tool
- a CRM
- a sales pipeline tool
- a content network
- an attention business

The simplest distinction:

**Qooee is for keeping in touch, not being seen.**

## 2. Naming System

### Master Brand

Use Qooee.

Do not write:

- QOoee
- QoOee
- QOOEE, except where system constraints require uppercase

### Product Name

Use Qooee Contacts for the first product unless a formal naming decision changes it.

Good:

- Qooee Contacts helps professionals keep trusted contact details current.
- Get Qooee Contacts.
- Sign in to Qooee.

Avoid mixing product names in public copy. Pick one product name per release and use it consistently.

### Name Meaning

Qooee can be framed lightly as a call across distance. It suggests staying within reach.

Use this sparingly.

Good:

**Qooee is about staying within reach.**

Avoid:

**Inspired by the iconic Australian bush call, Qooee reimagines connection for the modern professional era.**

The meaning should support the brand. It should not become the whole brand story.

## 3. Brand Personality

Qooee should feel:

- quiet: confident without shouting, helpful without demanding attention
- competent: well-made, dependable, and thought through
- pragmatic: solving a real problem directly
- respectful: respecting time, privacy, attention, and relationships
- lightly enjoyable: pleasant through clarity, calmness, and small moments of polish

Qooee should make users feel organised, relieved, in control, quietly confident, and less burdened by contact admin.

It should not aim to make users feel excited, popular, addicted, or socially validated.

## 4. Tone Of Voice

### Voice Principles

Say what it does. Lead with function before concept.

Good: **Keep your contacts current.**

Bad: **Reimagine the future of professional connection.**

Use plain language. Prefer contacts, details, keep in touch, connection, trusted, current, shared, and control.

Avoid inflated terms unless genuinely needed: ecosystem, engagement, platform, community, growth loop, relationship intelligence, and social graph.

Stay calm. Do not create fake urgency or use inflated claims.

Respect the user. Do not over-explain simple ideas and do not use motivational productivity language.

### Copy Examples

Good phrases:

- Keep your contacts current.
- A quieter way to keep in touch.
- Professional contact details, kept up to date.
- No feeds. No posting. No CRM clutter.
- Useful networks, not social noise.
- Contact details should not go stale.
- Stay reachable without the upkeep.

Bad phrases:

- Supercharge your professional network.
- Unlock relationship-driven growth.
- Build your personal brand effortlessly.
- Reimagine professional engagement.
- Join the future of connected productivity.
- Grow your network like never before.

### Capitalisation

Use sentence case for interface copy and body copy.

For marketing headlines, short lowercase headlines may be used when they suit the layout and the wordmark. This helps the brand feel calmer and more distinctive.

Examples:

- keep your contacts current
- a quieter way to keep in touch
- no feeds. no posting. no crm clutter.

Use title case only for product names, document titles, App Store fields, and contexts where the format expects it.

## 6. Colour System

The supplied `.clr` palette files in [palettes](palettes) are the canonical brand colours. Do not replace these colours with an alternative palette.

### Primary Palette

| Name | Hex | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Honey Yellow | `#FEAE00` | Signature accent, wordmark colour, warmth, selected highlights |
| Rich Black FOGRA 29 | `#001524` | Primary dark, Q mark, headings, navigation, dark surfaces, main CTA |
| Isabelline | `#F6F0ED` | Primary light background, inverse logo colour, warm neutral surface |
| Ruby Red | `#A20021` | Strong emphasis, critical states, rare expressive accent |

Rich Black FOGRA 29 is the main brand anchor. Use it instead of pure black wherever possible.

Honey Yellow is the signature accent. Use it for the wordmark, small accents, icons on navy, small highlights, and selected states where contrast is sufficient. Do not use it for small text on Isabelline or white.

Isabelline is the main quiet background. It makes the brand feel warmer than a pure white software interface.

Ruby Red is emotionally strong. Use it for destructive actions, critical alerts, important warnings, and rare editorial emphasis.

### Secondary Palette

| Name | Hex | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Steel Blue | `#3F7CAC` | Information, links where appropriate, calm supporting accent |
| Silver Pink | `#C1A5A9` | Soft human warmth, secondary surfaces, illustration support |
| Xanadu | `#667761` | Muted green-grey, quiet confirmation, calm secondary emphasis |

Use secondary colours to support meaning and provide visual variety. Check contrast before using Steel Blue or Xanadu for small text. Do not use Silver Pink for body text.

### Tertiary Palette

| Name | Hex | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Honey Yellow 30% | `#FFE7B3` | Soft highlight background |
| Honey Yellow 15% | `#FFF3D9` | Very light warm tint |
| Ruby Red 30% | `#FF97AC` | Strong error or alert tint, sparingly |
| Ruby Red 15% | `#FFCBD5` | Soft error or alert background |
| Steel Blue 30% | `#C3D8E9` | Information background |
| Steel Blue 15% | `#E1ECF4` | Very light information background |
| Platinum | `#ECE4E5` | Neutral surface, divider, card fill |
| Light Gray | `#D0D7CE` | Muted surface, inactive state, subtle border |

Use tertiary colours for panels, badges, subtle backgrounds, status surfaces, diagrams, empty states, and product illustrations. Do not use tertiary tints as main text colours.

### Colour Proportions

For marketing pages:

- 60 to 70 percent Isabelline, white, and warm neutrals
- 20 to 30 percent Rich Black FOGRA 29
- 5 to 10 percent Honey Yellow
- 2 to 5 percent secondary and tertiary colours
- Ruby Red only when the message genuinely needs force

For product UI:

- neutral surfaces should dominate
- Rich Black FOGRA 29 should carry structure and action
- Honey Yellow should be used less often than the logo suggests
- secondary colours should support meaning, not decorate every screen

### Accessibility Rules

- Use Rich Black FOGRA 29 on Isabelline for primary text.
- Use Isabelline or white on Rich Black FOGRA 29 for dark surfaces.
- Use Honey Yellow on Rich Black FOGRA 29 for strong branded accents.
- Do not use Honey Yellow as small text on Isabelline or white.
- Do not use Silver Pink as text on light backgrounds.
- Check Steel Blue and Xanadu before using them for small text.
- For primary buttons, prefer a Rich Black FOGRA 29 background with Isabelline or white text.
- For warning and error text, prefer Ruby Red on light backgrounds, not Ruby Red tints.

### Recommendation

Keep the current logo and wordmark.

The mark is distinctive, readable, and has enough character to avoid looking like a generic contacts or SaaS app. The open Q symbol fits contact, reach, and connection without overused network-node imagery.

Do not redesign the logo for the sake of making it more conventional.

### Logo Colours

Primary wordmark:

- Q mark: Rich Black FOGRA 29 `#001524`
- "ooee": Honey Yellow `#FEAE00`
- background: transparent, Isabelline, or white

Inverse wordmark:

- Q mark: Isabelline `#F6F0ED`
- "ooee": Honey Yellow `#FEAE00`
- background: Rich Black FOGRA 29 or another dark surface

Do not call the inverse mark the white logo in brand documentation. It is the inverse cream wordmark.

### Icon Mark

Use the Q symbol alone only when space is limited or the brand is already known.

Best uses:

- app icon
- favicon
- profile avatar
- compact navigation
- loading state
- small watermark

Avoid using the icon alone for first exposure if the user may not know the brand.

### App Icon Direction

Preferred app icon:

- Isabelline square background
- Rich Black FOGRA 29 Q mark
- no text
- no amber unless needed as a very small future accent

Optional dark mode app icon:

- Rich Black FOGRA 29 square background
- Isabelline Q mark

Do not use Honey Yellow as the full app icon background.

### Production Cleanup

The current wordmark SVGs reference Crete Round as live text. If the font is missing, the wordmark may render incorrectly.

Crete Round is available from [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Crete+Round). A repo copy is included at `assets/fonts/CreteRound-Regular.ttf`, with its SIL Open Font Licence at `assets/fonts/OFL.txt`.

For production use:

- export the wordmark as outlined vector paths
- keep an editable master file separately
- distribute only outlined SVG, PDF, and PNG assets
- treat the font as a logo-production dependency, not a general brand typeface

### Clear Space And Minimum Size

Use the thickness of the Q stroke as the minimum clear space around the wordmark.

For the icon-only mark, use at least one stroke width around all sides.

Recommended minimums:

- full wordmark: 120 px wide on screen
- icon-only mark: 24 px wide on screen
- favicon: test manually at 16 px and 32 px

At very small sizes, use the icon-only mark rather than forcing the full wordmark.

### Incorrect Use

Do not:

- recolour the Honey Yellow wordmark letters
- change the Q mark to an arbitrary colour
- use pure black when the brand navy is available
- use pure white as a substitute for Isabelline unless forced by a technical constraint
- add gradients
- add drop shadows
- stretch, compress, or rotate the mark
- recreate the wordmark with typed text
- place the full wordmark below 120 px wide
- put the logo over busy imagery

## 8. Typography

The wordmark uses Crete Round, a rounded slab typeface available from Google Fonts. Keep this character inside the logo. Do not make Crete Round the general brand typeface.

Recommended main type family: Inter.

Why:

- excellent UI readability
- strong performance in lists, forms, and contact cards
- neutral enough to let the logo carry personality
- dependable on web and product surfaces
- does not compete with the rounded wordmark

For iOS, use SF Pro in product UI unless there is a strong reason not to. For web and cross-platform surfaces, use Inter.

Recommended web stack:

```css
font-family: Inter, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "SF Pro Text", "Segoe UI", sans-serif;
```

Hierarchy:

- marketing headline: Inter Display or Inter SemiBold, large, calm, spacious, not overly heavy
- section heading: Inter SemiBold, concise, sentence case or short lowercase
- body: Inter Regular, comfortable line height
- UI labels: Inter Medium or SF Pro Medium, short and precise

Type rules:

- do not overuse bold
- do not use all caps for routine UI
- keep line lengths comfortable
- use generous but controlled line height
- let the logo provide brand character and let product text stay clear

## 9. Layout And Visual System

Qooee should feel calm, orderly, clear, professional, lightly warm, and quietly enjoyable.

It should not feel loud, playful, corporate-grey, social-media-like, or dashboard-heavy.

### Spacing

Use generous spacing, but avoid luxury-brand emptiness. The product should feel efficient. Large gaps should have a reason.

| Token | Value |
| --- | --- |
| `space.1` | 4 px |
| `space.2` | 8 px |
| `space.3` | 12 px |
| `space.4` | 16 px |
| `space.5` | 24 px |
| `space.6` | 32 px |
| `space.7` | 48 px |
| `space.8` | 64 px |
| `space.9` | 96 px |

### Corners

Use medium rounding:

- cards: 16 px
- buttons: 12 px
- small controls: 8 px
- app icon: follow platform conventions

Avoid cartoonish pill shapes everywhere.

### Shadows

Use shadows sparingly. Prefer subtle borders, quiet elevation, and soft surface contrast. Avoid large floating SaaS cards, heavy drop shadows, and glassmorphism.

### Iconography

Use simple line icons with rounded ends where appropriate. Icons should be functional, quiet, and legible at small sizes.

Avoid generic network-node icons, handshakes, megaphones, follower symbols, trophy icons, and status theatre.

### Illustration

Illustration is optional. If used, keep it abstract, sparse, geometric but warm, and based on the Q mark, circles, contact cards, and clean lines.

Do not use lifestyle stock photography to explain the product.

## 10. UI Direction

Product design principle:

**The product should make contact maintenance feel handled, not exciting.**

Main UI principles:

- contact first: the person and their details should be the focus
- clear update status: show which details are current, which changed, who changed them, and what is shared back
- avoid notification noise: only notify users when a change matters
- calm actions: use direct verbs such as Connect, Share details, Update my details, Review change, and Stop sharing
- visible privacy: when sharing details, show exactly what is shared and with whom

Good notification:

**Alex Chen updated their work email.**

Bad notification:

**Your network is moving. See what changed today.**

## 11. Component Style Guidance

### Buttons

Primary button:

- Rich Black FOGRA 29 background
- Isabelline or white text
- use for account creation, saving, confirming, and major product actions

Secondary button:

- transparent or Isabelline background
- navy text
- subtle border
- use for secondary navigation, cancellation, and supplementary actions

Accent button:

- use Honey Yellow rarely
- pair Honey Yellow background with Rich Black FOGRA 29 text
- check contrast carefully

Do not make every CTA yellow.

### Cards

Cards should be clean and information-led:

- Isabelline or white background
- Platinum or Light Gray border
- 16 px radius
- minimal shadow or no shadow

### Forms

Forms should feel simple and trustworthy:

- clear labels
- direct helper text
- visible privacy context where relevant
- strong focus states using navy or Steel Blue

Avoid placeholder-only labels, novelty inputs, and excessive helper copy.

### Badges And Status Labels

Use tints from the tertiary palette.

Examples:

- current: Xanadu or Steel Blue tint, depending on context
- changed: Honey Yellow tint
- review needed: Honey Yellow 30%
- error: Ruby Red 15% background with Ruby Red text

Keep status language plain:

- Current
- Updated
- Review
- Not shared
- Private

Avoid status language such as Hot, Trending, Boosted, and Popular.

## 12. Imagery And Product Visuals

Use real product screens wherever possible.

Best hero visual:

- a clean contact detail screen
- visible current-status indicator
- one subtle update example
- clear controls for shared details

Screenshots should be real or close to real, lightly framed, shown on Isabelline or navy surfaces, and not over-annotated. Use callouts below screenshots rather than floating labels everywhere.

Avoid fake charts, fake company logos, fake social proof, generic people photos, abstract 3D blobs, illustrated crowds, and performative networking imagery.

## 13. Motion

Motion should support comprehension only.

Good uses:

- a contact update changing state
- a connection request being accepted
- a shared detail becoming current
- subtle transitions between screens

Avoid confetti, celebratory bursts, looping hero animations with no informational purpose, and distracting background movement.

## 14. Product Philosophy

Decision principles:

- useful before visible
- calm before addictive
- trust before growth
- accuracy before abundance
- professional before performative
- simple before clever

Do not build:

- feeds
- likes
- reactions
- comments
- public follower counts
- public posting
- ranking systems
- streaks
- engagement badges
- growth prompts
- people-you-may-know spam
- content discovery
- heavy CRM pipelines
- lead scoring
- public popularity signals

Some of these may look tempting for growth. They would weaken the brand.

## 15. Differentiation

Versus LinkedIn:

**LinkedIn is for being seen. Qooee is for staying reachable.**

Versus Apple Contacts and Google Contacts:

**Contacts apps store information. Qooee helps maintain it.**

Versus CRM tools:

**A CRM manages leads. Qooee looks after contacts.**

Examples:

## 19. Key Recommendations

Keep:

- the current Q mark
- the current wordmark
- the supplied primary palette
- the supplied secondary palette
- the supplied tertiary palette
- the quiet professional positioning
- the restrained, useful tone

Fix:

- productionise the logo files by outlining the wordmark text
- keep semantic logo filenames
- control Honey Yellow usage
- standardise the public product name as Qooee Contacts

Do not change:

- do not redesign the brand just to make it look more conventional
- do not make Honey Yellow the dominant product UI colour
- do not turn Qooee Contacts into a social network, feed, CRM, or engagement product

The current mark and palette have useful personality. The brand will feel mature if the surrounding system is disciplined, calm, and consistent.
